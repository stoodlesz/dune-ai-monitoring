# Research Literature

This file tracks key papers and sources related to dune ecology, remote sensing, and environmental machine learning.  
It functions as a working reading log for the project.

## Coastal Dune Ecology

### Psammosere Succession

Ranwell, D.S. (1972). *Ecology of Salt Marshes and Sand Dunes.* London: Chapman and Hall.

Key points

- classic ecological description of dune succession
- defines major psammosere stages
- explains vegetation colonisation patterns in coastal dunes

Possible use in project

- defining classification categories for AI models

---

### European Dune Systems

Doing, H. (1985). Coastal fore-dune zonation and succession in various parts of the world. *Vegetatio*, 61, pp.65–75.

Key points

- global comparison of dune succession patterns
- vegetation zonation models

Possible use

- defining ecological labels for classification datasets

---

## Remote Sensing for Ecosystem Monitoring

Pettorelli, N. (2013). *The Normalized Difference Vegetation Index.* Oxford: Oxford University Press.

Key points

- NDVI as a vegetation monitoring metric
- widely used in satellite ecology

Possible use

- vegetation index calculation from satellite imagery

---

Xie, Y., Sha, Z. and Yu, M. (2008). Remote sensing imagery in vegetation mapping: a review. *Journal of Plant Ecology*, 1(1), pp.9–23.

Key points

- overview of vegetation classification techniques
- satellite data processing workflows

Possible use

- baseline methodology for classification models

## Environmental AI

Reichstein, M. et al. (2019). Deep learning and process understanding for data-driven Earth system science. *Nature*, 566, pp.195–204.

Key points

- integration of machine learning and environmental science
- limitations of purely data-driven ecological models

Possible use

- framing the research contribution
